1.3 Solid Waste Management

Regular waste management, including garbage collection, recycling programs, and proper waste disposal, ensures clean streets and minimizes health risks.

Garbage Collection

Garbage collection refers to the organized process of collecting, transporting, and disposing of waste materials generated by households, businesses, and public spaces within a city or town. It is a critical public service managed by local governments or contracted private companies to maintain cleanliness, public health, and environmental sustainability.

Recycling Programs

Municipal recycling programs are government-run efforts designed to collect, process, and repurpose waste materials that can be recycled. These programs are often managed by municipal authorities or contracted companies, ensuring that recyclable materials are diverted from landfills and channelled into reuse or manufacturing processes. From curbside pickup to designated drop-off centers and specialized facilities, municipalities develop systems tailored to their population's needs and resources.

Waste Disposal

As the population grows, so does the amount of garbage a community generates. Reusing and recycling have helped reduce a lot of waste. However, there’s still a lot of garbage that the municipality needs to deal with. Landfills, areas dedicated to the collection and burying of waste, are usually the answer to this.
