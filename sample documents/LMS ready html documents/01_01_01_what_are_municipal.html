1.1 What are Municipal Services?

Municipal services are the foundation of Canadian communities. They provide important infrastructure, safety, and support for economic and social well-being. Cities, towns, and rural municipalities manage services like clean water, waste management, public safety and recreation. Understanding these services is important for effective municipal administration.

In the image below, municipal services are grouped into six categories. Expand each category to see what’s included in each area. In the following screens, we’ll explore each category in more detail.

Text Version of Visual

Water Supply and Sewage Management: Provision of safe, clean water to the community, land drainage systems, and treatment and disposal of wastewater.

Solid Waste Management: Collection, treatment, recycling, and disposal of solid waste.

Public Safety and Emergency Services: Police, fire and emergency services such as paramedics, and emergency management agencies.

Parks and Recreation: Recreational facilities and programs, public libraries.

Transportation Infrastructure: Road maintenance, public transportation systems, traffic management.

Economic Development: Initiatives like small business attraction and support programs, as well as tourism.
